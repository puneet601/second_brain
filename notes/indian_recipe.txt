### **1. Paneer Butter Masala**

A luxurious North Indian curry featuring soft cubes of paneer simmered in a velvety tomato-butter gravy. The sauce is made with pureed tomatoes, cream, butter, and a blend of mild spices like garam masala, kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), and cardamom.
**Serving Tip:** Best enjoyed with naan or jeera rice.
**Variation:** Add a hint of honey or sugar to balance the tanginess of the tomatoes.

---

### **2. Chicken Tikka Masala**

Succulent chicken pieces are marinated overnight in yogurt, lemon juice, and spices like cumin, coriander, and paprika, then grilled or roasted before being added to a creamy tomato-based masala sauce.
**Flavor Notes:** Smoky, tangy, and creamy all at once.
**Serving Tip:** Serve with garlic naan or steamed basmati rice.
**Variation:** Try replacing chicken with paneer or tofu for a vegetarian version.

---

### **3. Dal Makhani**

A Punjabi delicacy made from whole black lentils (urad dal) and red kidney beans (rajma), slow-cooked overnight or pressure-cooked until soft, then simmered in butter, cream, onions, and tomatoes for hours to achieve its signature depth of flavor.
**Texture:** Thick, creamy, and comforting.
**Serving Tip:** Perfect with rice, naan, or paratha.
**Variation:** For a vegan version, substitute cream with coconut milk and butter with vegan margarine.

---

### **4. Chole Masala**

A hearty chickpea curry from Northern India made by cooking boiled chickpeas in a spiced onion-tomato gravy flavored with ginger, garlic, and chole masala powder.
**Flavor Profile:** Spicy, tangy, and slightly sour from amchur (dry mango powder).
**Serving Tip:** Serve with bhature, puri, or basmati rice.
**Variation:** Add a spoon of tamarind pulp or anardana (pomegranate seeds) for extra tang.

---

### **5. Aloo Gobi**

A comforting vegetarian classic made by saut√©ing potatoes and cauliflower with onions, tomatoes, turmeric, cumin, and coriander powder.
**Texture:** Dry-style curry, perfect for everyday meals.
**Serving Tip:** Tastes great with roti or dal-rice combo.
**Variation:** Add green peas for color and sweetness.

---

### **6. Rajma Masala**

Kidney beans slow-cooked in a spiced tomato-onion gravy until the flavors deeply infuse into the beans.
**Flavor Notes:** Earthy, mildly spicy, and rich in protein.
**Serving Tip:** Traditionally served with steamed rice (Rajma-Chawal), a North Indian comfort meal.
**Variation:** Add cream or yogurt for a thicker consistency.

---

### **7. Mutton Curry / Rogan Josh**

An aromatic curry made with tender goat or lamb meat cooked in a base of onions, tomatoes, and whole spices. The Kashmiri version, **Rogan Josh**, uses yogurt, Kashmiri red chili, and fennel for its deep red color and rich aroma.
**Serving Tip:** Enjoy with basmati rice or tandoori roti.
**Variation:** Slow-cook on low heat for 2‚Äì3 hours for melt-in-mouth tenderness.

---

### **8. Saag Paneer / Palak Paneer**

Soft cubes of paneer gently simmered in a smooth spinach puree infused with garlic, ginger, and garam masala.
**Flavor Notes:** Mild, wholesome, and nourishing.
**Serving Tip:** Pairs beautifully with roti, paratha, or jeera rice.
**Variation:** Add a spoon of cream or butter on top for a restaurant-style finish.

---

### **9. Biryani**

A royal, aromatic one-pot rice dish layered with marinated meat, caramelized onions, fragrant basmati rice, saffron, and spices like cardamom, cloves, and bay leaves.
**Types:** Hyderabadi (spicy and rich), Lucknowi (milder, fragrant), or Vegetable Biryani.
**Serving Tip:** Serve with raita or mirchi ka salan.
**Variation:** Replace meat with paneer, tofu, or soya chunks for a vegetarian option.

---

## ü´ì **Breads & Accompaniments**

### **1. Roti / Phulka**

A soft, unleavened flatbread made from whole wheat flour, rolled thin, and cooked on a griddle or directly over flame to puff up.
**Serving Tip:** Serve hot with any curry or dal.
**Variation:** Add ghee while serving for richer flavor.

---

### **2. Naan**

A fluffy, leavened flatbread made with refined flour and yogurt, traditionally cooked in a tandoor. Often brushed with butter or garlic butter.
**Serving Tip:** Pairs perfectly with creamy gravies like Paneer Butter Masala.
**Variation:** Try stuffed naans with cheese, garlic, or mint.

---

### **3. Paratha**

A layered, flaky flatbread made by folding and rolling dough, shallow-fried in ghee or oil. Can be plain or stuffed with potatoes, paneer, or vegetables.
**Serving Tip:** Enjoy with curd, pickle, or any curry.
**Variation:** Try **Lachha Paratha** for extra flakiness or **Methi Paratha** for a healthy twist.

---

### **4. Sambar**

A South Indian lentil stew prepared with toor dal (pigeon peas), tamarind, and vegetables like drumstick, pumpkin, or okra. The unique flavor comes from **sambar powder** ‚Äî a blend of roasted spices and lentils.
**Serving Tip:** Traditionally eaten with idli, dosa, or steamed rice.
**Variation:** Add coconut for a Kerala-style sambar.

---

A South Indian specialty ‚Äî a thin, crispy fermented crepe made from rice and lentil batter, filled with a spiced mashed potato mixture.

A flavorful street snack made by tossing fried or boiled potatoes with chutneys, onions, coriander, spices, and sometimes yogurt.
**Flavor Notes:** Tangy, spicy, and crunchy.
**Serving Tip:** Serve immediately to retain the crispiness.
**Variation:** Add pomegranate seeds or sev (crispy chickpea noodles) for extra texture.

---

### **3. Poha**

A light breakfast dish made from flattened rice saut√©ed with onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, and turmeric. Often garnished with coriander, lemon juice, and peanuts.

**Serving Tip:** Serve hot with chai.
**Variation:** Add peas or grated carrots for color and nutrition.
